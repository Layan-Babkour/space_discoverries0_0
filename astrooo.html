First Arab Muslim Astronaut

The first Muslim, and the first member of a royal family to travel into space. Prince Sultan bin Salman Al Saud is a Saudi royal, former astronaut, and government official. He became the first Arab, Muslim, and member of royalty to fly in space during the 1985 NASA Space Shuttle mission STS-51-G. He holds a degree in Communications and earned a PhD in History. Prince Sultan also served as the head of the Saudi Space Commission and was previously the president of the Saudi Commission for Tourism and National Heritage. His contributions to space exploration and heritage preservation are well-recognized.

Born 27 June 1956 (age 68)
Nationality Saudi Arabian
Occupation Chairman of the Board of Directors of the Saudi Space Commission
Parents Salman of Saudi Arabia (father), Sultana bint Turki Al Sudairi (mother)
