Explore the Stars

1. Star Formation
  1. Stars form in large clouds of gas and dust called nebulae.
  2. Gravity pulls the gas together to form dense regions where fusion can begin.
  3. Nuclear fusion of hydrogen atoms into helium generates energy and light.
2. Characteristics of Stars
  1. Brightness: Measured by apparent and absolute magnitude.
  2. Color: Blue stars are hot, red stars are cooler.
  3. Mass: Larger stars burn fuel faster and have shorter lifespans.
3. Types of Stars
  1. Dwarf Stars: Small stars like the Sun.
  2. Giant Stars: Larger and more luminous than dwarf stars.
  3. Supergiants: The largest and most luminous stars.
4. Star Evolution
  1. Stars begin as protostars, condensing from gas clouds.
  2. After hydrogen fuel is exhausted, stars expand into red giants or supergiants.
  3. Depending on the star’s mass, it may end as a white dwarf, neutron star, or black hole.
5. Famous Stars
  1. Sirius: The brightest star in the night sky, located in Canis Major.
  2. Betelgeuse: A red supergiant in the constellation Orion.
  3. Proxima Centauri: The closest star to Earth after the Sun.
